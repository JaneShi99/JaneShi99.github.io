Felix Zhou（周长风）

I am a CS PhD student at Yale University, where I mostly work in theory and ML.

Previously, I was an undergraduate student at the University of Waterloo.

Research

I had the pleasure of working with Chaitanya Swamy as an Undergraduate Research Assistant in Spring 2021. We explored the approximation algorithma for the minimum norm optimization problem.

I was fortunate to be supervised by Jochen Koenemann as a Undergraduate Research Assistant in Fall 2020. He, his PhD student Justin Toth, and I worked on computational game theory. We studied the computational complexity of nucleolus for cooperative b-matching games.

Publications

Jochen Koenemann, Justin Toth, Felix Zhou. "On the Complexity of Nucleolus Computation for Bipartite b-Matching Games". International Symposium on Algorithmic Game Theory, 2021.
