About me

I am a third year math PhD student at MIT, where I am extremely fortunate to be advised by Andrew Sutherland.

My main interest lies in computational number theory. In particular, I am interested in computing the L-functions of abelian surfaces.

Previously, I was a pure math masters student at the University of Cambridge with a focus in number theory.

Even before, I was an undergraduate student at the University of Waterloo studying a double major in CS and Pure Math. I was fortunate to be advised by David Jao. I worked on elliptic curve isogeny cryptography.

I am grateful to be supported by the NSERC Postgraduate Scholarship.

Personal: My better half, Felix Zhou, studies theoretical computer science at Yale.
